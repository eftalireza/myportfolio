About Me

I am a PhD student in Civil Engineering at the University of Galway, specializing in coastal engineering, marine energy, and oceanography. My work leverages advanced numerical modeling, high-performance computing, and high-resolution grid simulations to tackle complex renewable energy and environmental challenges.

Research Overview

My research focuses on developing innovative models that simulate tidal dynamics, wind and wave energy, and ocean currents. By integrating HPC and cutting-edge numerical simulation techniques, I optimize renewable energy extraction and enhance coastal resilience in the face of climate change.

Recent Projects

Here are some of the projects I have been involved in recently:

1. CROCO Model for Studying Currents and Climate Change Impacts (West Ireland)

Developing a state-of-the-art CROCO model to simulate ocean currents and evaluate climate change impacts along Ireland’s west coast. This project integrates high-resolution bathymetry, atmospheric forcing, and boundary conditions to accurately capture regional hydrodynamics and renewable energy potential.

2. Tidal Energy and Numerical Modeling

Using advanced models like DIVAST to study tidal dynamics and energy extraction potential in the Shannon Estuary, analyzing parameters such as sea surface height and depth-averaged velocities for accurate energy forecasting.

3. Wind and Wave Energy Analysis

Conducting comprehensive analyses of wind and wave data from Shannon Airport and other sources to evaluate renewable energy availability throughout the year in the Shannon Estuary region.

4. Machine Learning for Renewable Energy Forecasting

Developing predictive models using machine learning to enhance forecast accuracy for wind and tidal energy production while assessing climate change impacts on these resources.

5. Coastal and Marine Engineering Projects

Designing and teaching innovative courses such as Foundations of Marine & Coastal Engineering: Oceans Unleashed! at the University of Galway Youth Academy, introducing students to coastal protection, underwater engineering, and sustainable design.

6. Water Scarcity Research (Co-supervision)

Collaborating with Dr. Stephen Nash to investigate water scarcity in Iran through advanced statistical analysis and numerical modeling, focusing on the impacts of climate change and human activities.

7. Ocean Diplomacy and Marine Energy

Contributing to the Ocean Voices program at the University of Edinburgh, addressing capacity constraints in marine governance and amplifying the voices of ocean-dependent communities.

8. Environmental Impact and Data Integration

Integrating diverse environmental datasets—including satellite observations, remote sensing, and numerical simulations—to assess the impacts of climate change on tidal, wind, and wave energy systems for sustainable marine spatial planning.

9. Hydrodynamic 3D Modeling of Gorgan Bay

Developing a comprehensive 3D hydrodynamic model of Gorgan Bay to study its circulation patterns, wave behavior, temperature, salinity, and air pressure influences, providing critical insights for conservation and sustainable management of this biosphere reserve.

Publications

Characteristics of Shallow Flows in a Vegetated Pool—An Experimental Study

Examines turbulent flow structures and the influence of vegetation on shear stress and bursting events in gravel-bed river pools.

Turbulence Characteristics in Mild and Steep Entrance Slopes of Pool-Riffle Sequences

Investigates velocity and Reynolds stress distributions in pool-riffle sequences with varying entrance slopes, comparing laboratory and field data for improved river management.

Experimental Investigation of Scouring in Groups of Parallel Pipelines

Determines optimal spacing for submarine pipelines by analyzing scouring behavior, providing key insights for marine infrastructure design.

Weather Window Analysis for the Deployment, Operation, and Maintenance of Marine Renewable Energy Devices in Irish Coastal Waters

Evaluates the influence of wave height on site accessibility for MRE devices along Ireland’s coast using a 12-year met-ocean dataset.

3D Hydrodynamic Numerical Modeling of Gorgan Bay

Presents a 3D model of Gorgan Bay that examines circulation, temperature, and current dynamics, offering insights for biosphere reserve management.
